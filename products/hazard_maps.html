Comprehensive Hazard Mapping Technology

Our methodology for hazard mapping focuses on delivering precise seismic risk insights for both buildings and their contents by combining regional earthquake hazard data with detailed geotechnical information. The process begins with established seismic hazard datasets, which provide baseline hazard parameters—typically in terms of spectral acceleration or peak ground acceleration—across a wide range of return periods or earthquake scenarios. Where available, we also incorporate high-resolution, locally developed hazard maps to capture site-specific seismicity patterns more accurately.

A key enhancement to standard hazard mapping is our integration of soil amplification effects. Local soil conditions can significantly influence ground shaking intensity due to differences in soil type, density, and layering. To capture this, we use detailed geotechnical information, either from USGS soil classification data or from municipal and regional engineering surveys, to determine the amplification factors that modify the baseline hazard values. By applying these corrections, we ensure that the hazard levels are not only regionally representative but also tailored to the specific conditions of each site.

For contents coverage, we extend the hazard assessment to account for floor-level accelerations and dynamic amplification within buildings, providing insight into the vulnerability of non-structural elements and valuable assets.

The resulting hazard maps are scenario-based, allowing users to explore seismic risks under multiple earthquake return periods or design-level events. This multi-level approach improves decision-making in structural design, retrofitting strategies, insurance underwriting, and disaster preparedness. Ultimately, by merging macro-scale seismic hazard data with micro-scale soil response information, we deliver hazard maps that more accurately reflect the true seismic exposure, thereby enabling more effective risk mitigation for both buildings and their contents.
