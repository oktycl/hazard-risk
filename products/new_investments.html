Advanced Investment Planning Technology

The New Investments Tool leverages our hybrid AI algorithms and global-scale hazard datasets to provide investors, developers, and planners with data-driven predictions of construction costs for new building projects. By integrating worldwide seismicity maps and soil condition data with user-specified design parameters, the tool enables forward-looking cost estimation that reflects both geotechnical constraints and structural requirements.

The process begins with a simple interaction: users draw or select the footprint of their planned structure on the map and enter basic project details such as the number of stories and the intended building use. Our system then incorporates additional environmental and engineering parameters, including roofprint geometry, terrain slope, and soil classification, to assess conditions such as the need for deep excavation or specialized foundation systems. These factors are critical to cost estimation, as they directly influence both material requirements and construction methodology.

By merging this input with our hazard-aware geospatial data, the tool provides not only baseline cost predictions but also insights into potential risk-related cost drivers, such as structural system requirements in high-seismic regions or foundation challenges in weak soils.

For stakeholders, developers gain a rapid feasibility assessment for potential projects. Construction firms can use the outputs for preliminary budgeting and bid preparation. Financial institutions benefit from consistent, data-based insights when evaluating loans or investments in new developments. In summary, the New Investments Tool transforms minimal user inputs into comprehensive, site-specific cost predictions by combining geospatial intelligence, soil and hazard data, and hybrid AI modeling. This empowers decision-makers worldwide to evaluate project feasibility with greater accuracy, speed, and confidence before committing resources.
