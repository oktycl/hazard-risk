Privacy Policy

Hazard & Risk Technologies
Effective Date: August 25, 2025

Hazard & Risk ("Hazard & Risk Technologies") is a brand of MBYT Construction and Information Technologies LLC, based in Istanbul, Türkiye. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and protect your information globally, in compliance with applicable laws including GDPR, UK GDPR, CCPA/CPRA, and PIPEDA.

Article 1
SCOPE OF THIS POLICY

This Privacy Policy applies to all interactions with Hazard & Risk, including our website (hazardandrisk.com), applications, APIs, communication channels (emails, newsletters), social media pages, and offline interactions.

Article 2
INFORMATION WE COLLECT

We collect both Personal Information (that identifies you) and Other Information (that does not directly identify you).

Personal Information may include:

  • Name, company, role/title
  • Contact details (email, postal address, phone number)
  • Payment information (via third-party processors)
  • IP address and device identifiers

Other Information may include:

  • Device and browser data
  • Usage data, cookies, and analytics
  • Aggregated or anonymized information
Article 3
HOW WE COLLECT INFORMATION

We collect information:

  • Directly from you (forms, account creation, subscriptions)
  • Automatically (cookies, analytics, device logs)
  • From third parties (e.g., Google Analytics, business partners)
  • Offline interactions (meetings, conferences, workshops)
Article 4
HOW WE USE YOUR INFORMATION

We use collected data to:

  • Provide and maintain services
  • Process payments and subscriptions
  • Communicate with you (including newsletters and product updates)
  • Personalize user experience
  • Conduct analytics, research, and reporting
  • Comply with legal obligations
  • Ensure system security and fraud prevention
Article 5
SHARING OF INFORMATION

We may share information with:

  • Service providers and contractors supporting operations
  • Affiliates and business partners
  • Regulators and government authorities where legally required
  • In connection with corporate transactions (merger, acquisition, restructuring)

We do not sell personal information for monetary gain.

Article 6
SECURITY MEASURES

We apply organizational, technical, and administrative safeguards to protect your information. No system is fully secure; if you believe your data is at risk, contact us immediately.

Article 7
YOUR RIGHTS AND CHOICES

You may:

  • Opt-out of marketing emails at any time
  • Request access, correction, or deletion of your data
  • Request restriction of processing or object to certain processing
  • Manage your cookie preferences (where applicable)

Note: Administrative or service-related emails cannot be opted out of.

Article 8
COOKIES AND TRACKING TECHNOLOGIES

We use cookies and similar technologies (including Google Analytics) for:

  • Essential service functionality
  • Performance and usage measurement
  • Analytics and improvements

Users in the EU/UK will see a cookie consent banner and may manage or withdraw consent at any time.

Article 9
THIRD-PARTY SERVICES

Our services may link to or integrate with third-party platforms. For payments, we use Fastspring as our third-party payment processor. Their collection and use of your data is subject to their own privacy policies.

Article 10
INTERNATIONAL DATA TRANSFERS

We use AWS servers in both the United States and Europe. Where data is transferred outside the EU/UK, we rely on Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) and equivalent safeguards to ensure adequate protection.

Article 11
DATA RETENTION

We retain personal data only as long as necessary for the purposes described, or longer if required by law. Once no longer needed, data is securely deleted.

Article 12
GDPR & UK GDPR RIGHTS (EEA/UK USERS)

If you are located in the EU/UK, you have the following rights:

  • Right of access to your personal data
  • Right to rectification of inaccurate data
  • Right to erasure ("Right to be forgotten")
  • Right to restrict processing
  • Right to data portability
  • Right to object to processing (including direct marketing)
  • Right to withdraw consent at any time

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with your local Data Protection Authority.

Article 13
CCPA / CPRA RIGHTS (CALIFORNIA RESIDENTS)

California residents have the right to:

  • Know what data we collect and why
  • Access and request deletion of their personal data
  • Opt-out of data sharing categorized as "sale"
  • Be free from discrimination when exercising privacy rights
Article 14
PIPEDA (CANADA)

For Canadian residents, we comply with PIPEDA. You have the right to access, correct, and withdraw consent regarding your personal information, subject to legal limitations.

Article 15
USE BY MINORS

Our services are intended for professional use (B2B). We do not knowingly collect data from individuals under 18 years old. In the EU, parental consent is required for individuals under 16 years old.

Article 16
UPDATES TO THIS PRIVACY POLICY

This Privacy Policy may be updated periodically. The "Effective Date" above will always indicate the latest revision.
